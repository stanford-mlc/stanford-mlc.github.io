Research

1. Channel Estimation and Channel Prediction

We pioneer Neural Gaussian Radio Fields (nGRF)—a physics-aware framework that replaces ray tracing with explicit 3-D Gaussian primitives. nGRF renders full MIMO channel matrices in ≈1 ms and trains in minutes, delivering up to 10 dB SNR gains and reducing pilot overhead by >100×. A complementary continual-learning pipeline anchors parameters with replay, EWC, and synaptic-intelligence regularizers, enabling handover-robust CSI prediction across heterogeneous cells—all on NVIDIA GPUs.

2. Neural minPMAC — Non-Linear Precoder Design

Neural minPMAC is a learned surrogate that maps channel matrices directly to optimal minPMAC decoding orders and power allocations for multicarrier NOMA uplinks. By distilling thousands of CVX-derived solutions into a compact network, it eliminates iterative convex solvers and brings real-time, inference-only non-linear precoding to embedded and cloud RAN accelerators.

3. CUDA minPMAC — GPU-Accelerated Precoder Solver

For scenarios where exact convex optimality is required, we implement a custom CUDA minPMAC solver. By parallelizing matrix factorizations and KKT updates across tens of thousands of CUDA cores, the GPU version achieves >100× speed-ups over CPU-based CVX, enabling millisecond-scale scheduling loops for 5G NR and Wi-Fi 7 access points.

4. Machine Learning for Hybrid 5G Digital Twin

We fuse nGRF-based channel engines, Neural minPMAC, and CUDA minPMAC inside a GPU-native hybrid digital twin built atop NVIDIA Sionna and Aerial. The twin streams differentiable RF simulations into reinforcement-learning agents, jointly optimizing beamforming, spectrum slicing, and traffic steering across terrestrial and non-terrestrial segments. This closed-loop platform accelerates 6G research by allowing “train-in-twin, deploy-in-RAN” experimentation with full PHY-to-MAC fidelity.
